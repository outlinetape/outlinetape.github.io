About Outline


Outline is a broadcast on Tilos Radio (an alternative radio station on 90.3 FM Budapest, Hungary that has been broadcasting since the '90s) every other thursday from 1 to 3 am.

In 2015 it started as an outsider electronic music broadcast with the main focus of bedroom techno, what with time become even more free and colorful show where many types of obscure music is warmly welcomed.

About me


Yinna is a well known DJ in the Budapest nightlife, who has many faces, playing mainly outsider house before focusing on techno and it's more experimental side.

She is the curator and a broadcaster of Outline on Tilos Radio and also on Rolo FM. Her radio shows are notoriously eclectic and always showcasing an obscure range of music. A member of the Farbwechsel label, half of the feminist DJ collective A C I D W I T C H, an organiser and performer at the Eclispe events.

An occasional visitor around Europe, you may know her from performances at Lecken in Berlin and Dunkle Vorahnung in Hannover. Yinna is spending the lockdown experimenting with synthesis as she is lost recently in the Eurorack rabbit hole. She hopes to return to Germany when she starts performing again.

About Tilos Radio


Tilos Rádió was the first community radio station in Hungary, established as a pirate broadcaster in 1991. The station's programmers have always contributed on a voluntary basis. Tilos Rádió has never broadcast advertisements, and has a strong social and freedom of expression commitment.

Tilos Rádió was established as a pirate community radio station in Budapest in 1991 as a way of bringing the public's attention to the fact that there was at that time no legal framework for independent and community broadcasters. During the first years of its broadcasting, Tilos (meaning "forbidden" in Hungarian) enjoyed wide public interest and played a key role in the 1995 liberalisation of the airwaves in Hungary.

Tilos Rádió obtained a legal frequency license in 1995 and became a popular local community station in Budapest with its morning phone-in talk-shows and its music programmes, broadcasting 12 hours a day. Tilos is a key player in the cultural and lifestyle scene of Budapest. Meanwhile, Tilos Rádió has its fingers on the public pulse with its social thinking, minority oriented programmes, and its radical and tolerant attitude. Tilos' broadcasts are mainly financed by listeners' donations and the income from fund-raising events, and partly by support from EU programmes, international NGOs and charity institutions.
